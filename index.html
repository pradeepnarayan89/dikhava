Access to Opportunity

Our current standardized education system has managed to build multiple barriers to entry for the youth in the form of test scores & high fee structures all of which generally require a certain degree of privilege. Even worse, more than 33% of who cross these barriers today, don’t get any job. A lot more work on sub standard jobs where there degree isn’t useful.

Current College System

Colleges as they exist today, stand disconnected with the Industrial reality. They are following curriculums set in 90s well into the 21st century leading to archaic knowledge and skillset of the youth.
Essential skills of problem solving & critical thinking are ignored in the mass of Indian colleges. A lot of skills being taught has no relevance and use in real life or industry.

Employers' Dilemma

After filtering out perspective employees from high noise to signal ratio environments, employers end up with candidates who require training. The six months training focuses on content that the students should have learnt in colleges, giving a sense of efficacy of our colleges today. Such a process is not only expensive, but also lead to a lot of time delays and unpreditability for the Employers.
