ABOUT US

Access to Opportunity

Our current standardized education system has managed to build multiple barriers to entry for the youth in the form of test scores & high fee structures all of which generally require a certain degree of privilege.

More than 33% of who cross these barriers today, don’t get any job. A lot more work on sub standard jobs where their degree isn’t useful.

Current College System

Colleges as they exist today, stand disconnected with the Industrial reality. They are following curriculums set in 90s in the 21st century leading to archaic knowledge and skillset of the youth.

Essential skills of problem solving & critical thinking are ignored in the mass of Indian colleges. A lot of skills being taught today has no relevance and use in real life or industry.

Employers' Dilemma

After filtering out perspective employees from high noise to signal ratio environments, employers end up with candidates with half baked understanding or none at all. The six months training focuses on content that the students should have learnt in colleges, expressing the failure of our colleges today.

Such a process is not only expensive, but also lead to a lot of time delays and unpreditability for the Employers.

Theory of Change - I

In the short-term, our students will be equipped with skills to pursue aspirational jobs like software engineering. They can choose to join the mainstream workforce or stay back in their communities and work remotely on opportunities which will be made available to them. They will become socio-economic centers in their families and communities.

Theory of Change - II

In the long-term, our students will be using their skills to solve the problems that are plaguing their communities. From making simple apps to learn english to social network for farmers, there is a big role our alumni will play to bring technology to their communities, Our alumni will be an inspiration and examples for locals to understand how learning and opportunities are getting reinvented through Internet.

CONTRIBUTE

While mobilising students for our first batch, we met exceptional students. Students who have great aptitude and hunger, but failed by our education system. Some of these are tenth dropouts, some others twelfth dropouts. Their parents can't afford the kind of education ours could. One sells tea, another works at a construction site, third drives Baxi (bike taxi), and fourth paints houses under a contractor. They can be trained to drive a Baxi, or maybe a Uber, but we believe that they deserve much better. To achieve this we need your help.
